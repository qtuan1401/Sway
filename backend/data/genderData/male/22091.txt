You’d think Hillary, Bill and Chelsea would want to use the national stage to tout how their family’s $2 billion foundation is “helping improve the lives of millions of people across the world,” as it boasts. You’d think we would’ve heard from some of the beneficiaries of the “partnerships between businesses, NGOs, governments and individuals” finding “solutions that last. ” But not a word that we can recall. No speeches, no videos, no personal testimonials  —   nothing. The silence is hardly mystifying  —   for the Clinton Foundation carries heavy baggage. Starting with the FBI’s investigation into whether any “intersection” between the foundation and the work of Secretary of State Clinton violated   laws. Like her role in handing Russia exclusive mining rights to 20 percent of US uranium reserves via a company that donated millions to the foundation.
