(Reuters)  —   Democratic presidential nominee Hillary Clinton’s lead over Republican rival Donald Trump narrowed to less than 3 percentage points, according to a   opinion poll released on Friday, down from nearly eight points on Monday. [About 42 percent of likely voters favored Clinton, to Trump’s 39 percent, according to the July  . 4 online poll of 1, 154 likely voters. The poll had a credibility interval of plus or minus 3 percentage points, meaning that the results suggest the race is roughly even.  Among registered voters over the same period, Clinton held a lead of five percentage points, down from eight percentage points on Monday, according to the poll.
