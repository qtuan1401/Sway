Beyoncé leads all artists with nine nominations at the 2017 Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year for her Black Lives   album “Lemonade,” while Rihanna, Drake, and Kanye West each earned eight nods and British pop queen Adele was tipped in top categories including Album of the Year for her commercial juggernaut 25. [Beyoncé’s Lemonade  —   initially released as a visual album drawing heavily from racial politics and black nationalist feminism  —   was nominated for Album of the Year along with Drake’s Views, Justin Bieber’s Purpose, Adele’s 25 and Sturgill Simpson’s major label debut A Sailor’s Guide to Earth.  Beyoncé also earned a Record of the Year nod for “Formation,” the upbeat anthem the singer performed at the Super Bowl halftime show, and the song’s video  —   which featured controversial imagery including the singer sitting on a sinking police car  —   also earned recognition for Video of the Year.   sensations Rihanna, Drake, and Kanye West wound up just behind Beyoncé, racking up eight Grammy nominations apiece. Beyoncé has 62 Grammy nominations across her   music career, including 20 wins, but has never won the coveted Album of the Year award. Meanwhile, British pop superstar Adele  —   already a   Grammy winner  —   grabbed nominations in three of the four major categories, including Album of the Year for 25 and Record of the Year and Song of the Year for her smash hit “Hello. ” Below is the full list of nominations, courtesy of Billboard.
